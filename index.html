    Sign Language Classifier

    Created a Convolutional Neural Network in Python to Classify American Sign Language Letters

    The sign language classifier was the main project for the Machine Learning in Python course I took in Spring 2020. To complete the project, our group created many different classifaction models for american sign language symbols, and compared their effectiveness. We tested the effectiveness of classifiers such as SVM, LDA + KNN, MLP, and random forest. In the end, we settled on using a convolutional neural network with three convolutional layers to classify the images. We chose to use a convolutional neural network (CNN) because of its ability to utilize locational information to process images. Our final CNN classifier had an accurary rating of around 90% for the letters a-h that we were tasked to classify.
